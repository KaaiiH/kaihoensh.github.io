Spring 2023

Teaching Assistant for Engineering Education 1120

Taking on the role of a Teaching Assistant (TA) for Engineering Education 1120 during the Spring of 2023 was a decision driven by my desire to support and encourage engineering students, especially women, to stay in the field. We need more women in STEM, and I wanted to do my part in fostering an inclusive and supportive environment for all students. This role provided me with a platform to directly influence the academic journey of many young engineers and to advocate for gender diversity in the field.

One of the most challenging aspects of this position was the unexpected amount of grading. Initially, I underestimated the volume of assignments and exams that needed to be evaluated. It was a grueling task, often taking up more time than I had anticipated. Despite the tedium of grading, it was a critical part of the job, ensuring that students received the feedback they needed to improve. Balancing this workload with my own studies required a great deal of time management and dedication.

However, the true joy of the role emerged through my interactions with the first-year engineering students during class sessions. These interactions were the highlights of my week, offering a break from the monotony of grading. Engaging with them, understanding their challenges, and providing support was incredibly fulfilling. I relished the opportunity to mentor these students, helping them navigate both academic and personal hurdles. Their enthusiasm and determination were contagious and inspiring.

Getting to know the first-year engineers and helping them navigate their initial year problems was a highlight of my experience. I found it deeply rewarding to offer reassurance that things would improve and to assist them with their coursework. The personal connections I made and the impact I had on their educational journey were the most gratifying parts of being a TA. It was fulfilling to see their progress and to know that I had played a small part in their success.

The picture below was taken during one of our optional study tables, where I helped students with their ENED homework and concepts. These sessions were not only an opportunity to provide academic support but also a chance to build a sense of community among the students. It was during these study tables that I was able to address individual questions and concerns, fostering a supportive learning environment. The informal setting encouraged open dialogue and made it easier for students to seek help.
